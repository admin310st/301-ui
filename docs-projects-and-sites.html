Projects and Sites in 301.st

Use projects to organize workspaces and sites to structure the destinations and assets attached to them.

Why This Layer Matters

Domains alone are not enough for clean operations. Projects and sites keep related assets together so redirect work stays readable and scalable.

Projects

  • Logical workspaces for grouping domains, sites, and operational flows
  • Useful when different offers, brands, teams, or traffic clusters should stay separated
  • Better than managing everything from one flat account-wide list

Sites

  • Attach landing pages or operational destinations to projects
  • Keep downstream routing work understandable
  • Provide a cleaner base for redirect-linked workflows

A Practical Setup Pattern

  1. Start from a synced domain inventory
  2. Create projects around business or operational boundaries
  3. Add sites where routing or landing structure needs to stay explicit
  4. Only then apply redirect workflows

FAQ

  • Do I need a project for every domain?
    No. Projects should reflect operational groups, not force one-domain-per-project fragmentation.
  • Should I create sites before redirects?
    Only if your redirect workflow benefits from explicit site structure and organization.
  • Can I skip this layer?
    For small setups, sometimes. For growing operations, skipping it usually makes everything messier later.
