Domain Management in 301.st

A unified operations surface for synced domains, status review, filtering, and domain-level workflows.

What the Domains Layer Does

  • Aggregates domains from connected integrations
  • Helps operators review state without jumping across provider dashboards
  • Supports bulk actions for day-to-day maintenance
  • Feeds the rest of the product with domain inventory for projects, sites, and redirects

Typical Workflow

  1. Connect Cloudflare or registrar integrations
  2. Sync domain inventory into the domains page
  3. Filter and inspect operational state
  4. Attach domains to projects or downstream workflows
  5. Use redirects or site-linked flows from a clean domain inventory

Why Teams Use It

The value is not just storage. It is operational visibility: one table, one set of filters, one place to review and act.

How Domain Management Connects to the Rest of the Product

  • Projects: domains become part of organized workspaces
  • Sites: domains can be attached to site workflows
  • Redirects: domains are the base layer for redirect rollout

FAQ

  • Is this just a domain list?
    No. The value comes from filtering, inspection, and operational actions on top of synced inventory.
  • Do domains have to be in Cloudflare?
    Cloudflare is the main workflow, but registrar-connected inventory can still support management use cases.
  • Should I organize domains before redirects?
    Yes. A clean domain layer makes redirect operations much less error-prone.
